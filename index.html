Cybersecurity and Privacy

1. What does it do?


It is undeniable that privacy had been one of the most vital issues among people, businesses, and organizations even before the “digital big bang”. However, since the early days after the revolutionary invention of the computer and the arrival of the Internet, information security had become more important but more vulnerable and fragile since the information was transmitted to a digital platform instead of being in physical shape like before. Therefore, the term “Cybersecurity” had arrived, and it became the key to protecting and maintaining privacy, users’ assets, and classified information. In 2020, V. Michael and B. Ian noted in their book that cybersecurity covers a wide range of social and technical problems that must be taken into consideration to secure networked information systems and network privacy [1]. In addition, in 2021, according to A. Moallem, “Cybersecurity is a growing field of science composed of very diverse types of technologies used to protect systems from different kinds of attacks, some known and some unknown. Due to the complexity of the evolving aspects of these areas, there is no standard classification of protective technologies.” [2, p. 1].

The traditional approach of security to protect computer systems and network systems like authentication devices, firewalls, and virtual private networks (VPN) has a lot of different kinds of vulnerabilities since cyberattacks nowadays have become more advanced and sophisticated and they can disrupt security programs and infiltrate systems [3]. In the upcoming years, it is critical and escalating compulsory for companies and organizations to deal with complexity since the sprawling increase of today’s network scale is inevitable and clear borders, access points will be eliminated; IT teams can reach the point of exhaustion from relentless sprint of protecting information; more importantly, the volume of data will rise dramatically, which can give cybercriminals more thing to exploit, and “complexity is the enemy of security” [4]. Therefore, different technologies must be applied and integrated to upgrade cybersecurity so that it can resist modern cyberattacks.

Nowadays, as technology is growing rapidly, many existing tools and technologies can be used to enhance cybersecurity. There are two ways of categorizing cybersecurity technologies. The first way is based on the components that each technology is supposed to protect which are network, application, endpoint, data, identity, database, infrastructure, mobile, cloud, and backup [2, p.25]. The second way is the solution-based classification including Identity and Access Management, Risk and Compliance Management, Encryption, Data Loss Prevention (DLP), Unified Threat Management (UTM), Firewall, Antivirus/ Anti-malware Solutions, Intrusion Detection System (IDS)/ Intrusion Prevention System (IPS), Disaster Recovery, Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) Mitigation and Web filtering [2, p. 25].

Moreover, some specific technologies can be optimized for information protection. Firstly, data encryption is a very powerful way to protect confidential information from hackers. Moreover, this technology uses a symmetric-key algorithm and asymmetric-key algorithm such as Blowfish and RSA, respectively, to encode the sensitive data to meaningless language and this encoded data is only readable by the person having the decryption key [2, p. 30]. Secondly, biometric technology has developed significantly since it uses physical and behavioral traits which are permanent and unique for authentication, surveillance, and identification instead of conventional passwords, which is a very safe and reliable approach [2, p. 50]. Thirdly, firewall technology has been widely implemented in hardware and software from personal devices to large data systems in companies and organizations. In detail, a firewall is a network security system designed to monitor traffic to and from a computer or computer network and to block traffic that does not comply with security parameters in place so that it can protect a computer or computer network from cyberattacks [5]. In addition, malware protection technology is indispensable due to the proliferation of smart technologies and malware can be a significant threat. For instance, there were a lot of infamous malware attacks in the past like Stuxnet Worm (2010), Backoff (2014), or WannaCry Ransomware (2017), etc [2, p. 102-103]. Therefore, anti-malware solutions must be constantly conducted to detect and prevent malware from stealing confidential information. Finally, as we are living in a world of expanding connectivity, the Internet of Things technology will be a great cybersecurity approach. Moreover, IoT technologies connect all devices and make them “smart” by connecting them to other devices in a network and “intelligent” by using machine learning and data science to manage and analyze real data to make decisions and predict future events [2, p. 116]. In the future, as newer technologies will be introduced and developed, cybersecurity will implement those latest technologies to protect data and information.

2. What is the likely impact?


It is undeniable that cybersecurity will become an indispensable industry as the technology era continues to rise sharply. Thanks to cybersecurity, companies, organizations, or users, in general, will be more confident of information security and it may change how people think about information security. Furthermore, the concept of environmentalism will become ubiquitous among users since this is “a concept where humans believe in the responsibility to protect the planet and where norms of behavior have been established, nurtured, and generally followed.” [4, p. 284]. However, what we need in the technology era is “cyberspace environmentalism” [4, p. 284], which is a set of shared beliefs, norms, and shared experiences among people about the culture of security.

As a result, IT teams will not have to sprint endlessly to keep the data secured like before but then they have to come up with different approaches using a combination of different technologies like Data Science, Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence models and algorithms, etc to make cybersecurity systems more adaptive and keep the information systems safe from not only the old versions of cyberattacks but also the latest ones. According to Ken Xie, the CEO of Fortinet, “There is virtually no area of cybersecurity that won’t be transformed by AI and ML.” [4, p. 282] and Michael Xie, a president and the CTO of Fortinet, has claimed that “We must move from threat prevention to the greater challenge of securing hyperconnectivity.”

As the cybersecurity industry will need to implement various technologies, a diversity of professions will be in demand. For instance, if Machine Learning is adopted into cybersecurity, it will create some jobs in the Machine Learning field that specializes in cybersecurity or there will be cooperating activities between many teams to come up with approaches using different technologies. As a result, not only the information and data will become more secure, but tight-knitted solidarity will be built among teams in a company or an organization. On the contrary, there will be some old technologies will no longer be the main efforts in protecting the data, but they will become a part of the latest cybersecurity systems.

Internet of Things

1. What does it do?


It has been more than a decade since the phrase “Internet of Things” was first described by Kevin Ashton, a co-founder and executive director of the Auto-ID center, to reduce human workload in 1999 [6]. According to F. Xia, L. T. Yang, L. Wang, and A. Vinel, the Internet of Things refer to the networked interconnection of everyday objects, which are often equipped with ubiquitous intelligence and it will increase the ubiquity of the Internet by integrating every object for interaction through embedded systems, which leads to a highly distributed network of devices communicating with human beings as well as other devices [7]. Since then, IoT has become the future trend that most technologies are being developed around it.

Nowadays, IoT has appeared everywhere from conventional industries like healthcare, education, industrial manufacturing to modern industries using cutting-edge technologies like blockchain, cryptocurrencies, automation, and many more. The Internet of Things makes everything smarter and more helpful to our daily lives. For instance, recently, thanks to the Internet of Things, citizens can have a deeper understanding of the trend “smart homes” and they can experience it in person. In detail, smart homes, an application of IoT, are those where household devices can be monitored and controlled remotely, which can ease out the home automation task [8]. Moreover, “With the upcoming technologies like the IoT— both consumer and industrial— artificial intelligence, robotics, and evolving machine learning provides an open environment where potential business partners and their machines can collaborate and exchange information over a digitally connected network using cyber physical systems.” [9, p. 154].

During the COVID-19 pandemic and many safety protocols must be followed, the Internet of Things can be the key factor to make our lives better in the future. For example, there is a potential trend called “IoT-Based Smart Tourism” that can be applied to the reality that can help tourism to recover and rise again since this industry has been heavily damaged by the COVID-19 pandemic. According to W. Wang et al., “Efficient data transmission based on 5G technology and smart data processing based on AI technology are significant to unlocking IoT-based smart tourism applications” [10]. In addition, this technology can be taken even further and on a larger scale to turn such complex visions like smart cities into reality. Furthermore, R. Ramakrishnan and L. Gaur have claimed that “The IoT of today will extend to cover process, people, products, and other infrastructure to become Internet of Everything (IoE), thereby streamlining the manufacturing process.” [9, p. 154-155]. Moreover, IoT can be developed to make virtual worlds like the Metaverse that Facebook is doing which can be a turning point during the pandemic.

However, in order to upgrade and bring the Internet of Things to the next level, there are some more compulsory technologies and developments. Firstly, 5G technology is considered to be the most important upgrade that can help humans access more advanced technologies as it is far faster and more effective than recent 4G. According to J. Pisarov and G. Mester, “5G has brought three new aspects – higher speed, lower latency, and connection of multiple devices both as sensors and IoT devices” [11] and “Like other networks, 5G uses the cells system which separates territories into sectors and sends encoded signals between the hotspots using radio waves. Each cell must be connected to the network spine, whether wirelessly or through landlines. 5G works on higher frequencies than 4G, generally on two types of frequencies: bellow and above 6 GHz.” [11]. However, to reach the full potential of 5G technology, technology companies and organizations need an extra period of time for further research and development. Secondly, Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning are also necessary for IoT to grow since those two fields can make complex things more automated and simpler. In addition, when working with a huge volume of data since larger scale projects will be made, scientists and researchers will need assistance from Data Science.

2. What is the likely impact?


It is clear that the Internet of Things is becoming more and more vital and its impact on industries, organizations and our daily lives is inevitably huge, especially during the COVID-19 pandemic. Firstly, IoT combining with other technologies like Artificial Intelligence and 5G can be used to build from home automation, autonomous vehicles to smart cities. As a result, the quality of our lives will be sharply increased and there will be less pollution since exhaust fumes and waste will be strictly managed. Secondly, IoT can improve the supply chain, logistics, and other industries more efficiently by providing more detailed and up-to-date information, which can help companies to come up with more specific and more effective plans to increase their revenue [12]. Thirdly, in the healthcare industry, IoT sensors can be placed inside health monitoring equipment so that the patients’ health statistics can be updated frequently on the Internet for doctors, nurses, and even their families so that the treatment process and methodology will be improved considerably [13]. In addition, as more appliances will become smarter and well-managed by IoT systems, the consumed amount of energy and natural resources will drop, and it will save the environment from pollution and natural resources exhaustion. Most importantly, since the concept of the Internet of Things is interconnectivity, the cybersecurity systems should be generously invested, frequently updated and maintained to prevent personal information leakage, hackers, malwares, etc.

As the coverage of the Internet of Things will get larger over time, there will be more jobs available for people. For instance, technical jobs like developers, engineers that are relevant to Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, Data Science, Cybersecurity, and so on will be in high demand. Besides, it is necessary for people doing non-technical jobs to know more about the relevant technologies since they will use those technologies every day in their work. However, there will be fewer opportunities for uneducated or less-educated workers since automation will take over jobs requiring manual labour.

From my perspective, as a student, I am experiencing a few aspects of the Internet of Things in my daily life. For instance, due to the COVID-19 pandemic, students have been studying online through many platforms like Canvas, Microsoft Teams, etc. The Internet of Things has supported my studies with online video conferences so that I can join online lectures, ask questions to lecturers, and have nearly the same experience as face-to-face lectures. Besides, there are some other applications that I have experienced as a student such as online health declaration before going to the campus instead of paper declaration, attending online workshops, doing online tests, etc. Therefore, the Internet of Things will improve my daily life considerably and it will change people’s lives if it reaches its full potential. Moreover, there will be a lot of my household appliances will be automated and remotely controlled via the Internet. In addition, since self-drive vehicles will become ubiquitous and the vehicle systems will be kept up-to-date and well managed online, I will no longer experience rush hours and traffic congestions, especially in big cities of Vietnam, which can also reduce a significant amount of carbon dioxide emission to the environment.

The Internet of Things will also provide great support for my family and friends. For instance, household chores will be well done by automated machines which will be controlled online, and family members can have more time for their work and taking care of themselves. Since information will be moved online, my family members can work directly from home. As a result, the Internet of Things can be significantly and undeniably helpful for everyone in society.

Blockchain and Cryptocurrencies

1. What does it do?


In the vast world of IT, It is undeniable that Blockchain is the newest amongst the trend of evolving technologies. The discussion about this rising technology has been around recently as Metaverse has been introduced to people worldwide. To define the term “Blockchain technology”, Peter Mell stated in the article of Blockchain Technology Overview: “Blockchains are tamper evident and tamper resistant digital ledgers implemented in a distributed fashion usually without a central authority” [14]. Furthermore, Blockchain are defy as a decentralized transaction and data management technology in 2016 by Jesse Yli-Huumo [15]. In simple words, Blockchain are a technology where all the changes (codes, transactions, information,…) are resistant to tampering. By having no intermediary between transactions and using cryptography, information stored in a block of a blockchain will not be changed or modified by any of the sides.

Blockchain technology was discovered in 2008 along with the birth of Bitcoin. Since then, The technology was mostly linked to bitcoin and cryptocurrencies. However, with the rise of Bitcoin and the debut of Metaverse, Blockchain technology are on the rise to be one of the most well-known and dominant IT technologies in the future. As of now, Blockchain are being implied into different industries and become more and more useful to the world. Some applications of Blockchain that are worth mentioned are cryptocurrency, money transferring and most recently, Metaverse.

Currently, Blockchain is being in the phase where it is getting introduced to the audiences and users and is also being developed to become a worldwide sensations/trends. Meta are one of the leading company in bringing Blockchain application into more industries and getting people to be familiar to the technologies.

In the working of a blockchain, An input was modified by a technique called hashing. Sandeep Kumar Panda implies that with hashing, with an input type of whether text, file or image, the output is a fixed size alpha-numeric string [16]. With hashing, Blockchain allow users to transfer data without worry of being changed or tampered. The data which has been hashed will be stored in a block and users need to have a key to access the block. However, the block can only be accessed and cannot be modified or changed. When a person wants to make change to a block, they will have to create another the block which will stored all their modified data. Therefore, the old data will stay untouched.

Due to its security and decentralized features, Blockchain is not only used in just cryptocurrencies but also every transactions that are available online vary from renting a car, online voting to buying real estate and governance. Most recently, Blockchain has been announced to be used in the newest virtual world of Metaverse where people can live in with an avatar of themselves and do every trades/transactions and a wide range of other activities through blockchain technology.

“In general, the most common attraction of the blockchain is cryptocurrency (digital money). Nevertheless, research analysts from the government sector, corporate organizations, security institution as well as industrial segment are able to extend the potential of blockchain and its interests well beyond the domain of cryptocurrencies.”

Ahmad Firdaus, “The rise of “blockchain”: bibliometric analysis of blockchain study”, 2019

With a rapid rising of popularity and usability, blockchain is surely one a promising technology of the future and will play a major role in the future society.

Artificial Inteligience and Machine learning

1. What does it do?


The phrase Artificial Intelligence have been used since the 1950s. However, it was not until the 1980 that this term become commonly used. In a simple definition, Artificial Intelligence is the ability that allow computer to process tasks that are similar to human or represent what human are capable of. “If you can put knowledge into a program, then this program must be an AI program”, Roger C. Schank, 1987 [18] . To be more specific, a code are made to solve problems from simple to complex, a simple code solve the problem by follow the guidance of a coder. On the other hand, AI used the technique that are code by the programmers to learn and develop a solution for the problem.

AI are also divided into three main types which commonly known as artificial narrow intelligence, artificial general intelligence and artificial super intelligence. For artificial narrow intelligence (also known as weak AI), the programmed AI are capable of doing task in one specific field. The artificial general intelligence, on the other hand, are able to perform a much more complicated task which required multiple knowledge from different field. There was a AI robot named Sofia which take the internet by storm by the way it looks and the way it gained a citizenship in Saudi Arabia, the program that made up the intelligence of the robot is what considered as artificial general intelligence (strong AI). The third type of AI – artificial super intelligence – which is also known as Super AI have not been well-implemented into the real world as it is still in developing phase. However, super AI are hoped to be able to complete tasks that human has never been done and its work will be way greater than what humanity are capable of. With the creation of super AI, humanity will be able to reach a greater achievement.

Nowadays, AI are being used in most of people’s life, its usage are numerous with functionalities vary from personal assistant to even managing a production chain in a factory. A close example of it is the personal assistant that most people interact with everyday is the personal assistant which is a built-in function in people’s phone, computer,…. Even though personal assistants are not common in Vietnam, it has been widely used around the world for a long period of time. Some famous personal assistants are Google assistant, Amazon Alexa or Apple Siri.

As the demand for AI in daily life are increasing drastically, it is inevitable that AI will become the dominant topic and technology in the near future. As everyone has noticed, AI has already been used in many industries and are spreading more into more fields. However, AI only capable of doing a portion of all the tasks humanity are handling, which create a goal for all AI developers and the AI field to create an AI that can reach a level of intelligence that are capable of completing complex task and be able to take over human’s work, lessening the pressures and works on people’s shoulder.

2. What is the likely impact?


AI has been a hot topic and the goal for companies to aim for in a creation of their new products. With that, AI is set to be implied into people’s daily life and is going to be indispensable from human’s life in the future. With AI, human will be capable of doing difficult tasks and solving complex problems. Moreover, AI will reduce labor in many industrialized countries, replacing them with a better working force with. With the help of AI, companies are going to be boosted in their productions and expand their income rates. However, the downside of this, which was predicted several times in the past by the theoretical physicist Stephen Hawking, is that with the rise of AI, there will be a chance that most human work will be took over by AI, therefore creating a habit of relying on AI. Also, with the AI taking over the work of human, a numerous amount of worker will lose their job which will create labor shortages in several countries, harming that nation economically.
